Collect and organize information about your neighborhood with mobile or paper-based surveys.

We're building tools that let you collect information about your neighborhood better and faster.

  • Efficiency: Collect information on buildings, streets and places using a smartphone
  • Accessibility: Use a scannable paper survey instead of a mobile phone for hand-written information data collectors of all ages and technical ability can use.
  • Visualization: Easily export the results to Excel — or watch them appear in real time online.

Community groups organize to collect information about housing condition, vacancy, blight, urban gardens, and more. This process is time-consuming and frustrating: it can take months to develop the questions and process hundreds of pages of results.

A scannable paper survey: everyone can participate.

Not everyone has a smartphone. LocalData can generate printable surveys for you. Surveyors fill in bubbles to mark their answers. You scan the finished sheets (many libraries and universities have scanners).

To process the data, you upload the scanned documents to the website — results will appear almost immediately. No more months of waiting to process transcribed forms.

Modern mobile tools

Surveyors with smartphones (Androids and iPhones) and a data plan can collect data online out in the field. A simple interface makes it easy to for volunteers to submit good data fast.

Export the data, or view it live

You can download your data to Excel with the click of a button. And, you can watch results come in live from the smartphone survey.

You'll know when the data was submitted, and by whom, so you can control the quality of the data

Create your own surveys

We're working on a tool that will let you build your own custom survey online. It's not a SurveyMonkey replacement — instead, it gives you questions & surveys designed by data experts, plus the freedom to customize.

Developers: We'd love your help! Our code is on GitHub and our stories are in Pivotal Tracker. The mobile app is built on HTML5, Javascript, and Leaflet.
