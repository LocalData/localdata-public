LocalData

Tools to collect neighborhood information in your community.

Collect and organize information about your neighborhood with mobile or paper-based surveys.

  • Efficient: Collect information on buildings, streets and places using a smartphone
  • Accessible: Opt to use paper surveys, scan and transcribe pen-and-paper answers automatically
  • Visualize: Easily export the results to Excel — or watch them appear in real time online.

Community groups organize to collect information about housing condition, vacancy, blight, urban gardens, and more. This process is time-consuming and frustrating: it can take months to develop the questions and process hundreds of pages of results.

The LocalData project improves efficiency, data quality, and user experience without compromising accessibility or usefulness for data collectors of all ages and abilities.

A scannable paper survey: everyone can participate.

Surveyors fill in bubbles to mark their answers. You scan the finished sheets (many libraries and universities have scanners).

To process the data, you upload the scanned documents to the website — results will appear almost immediately. No more months of waiting to process transcribed forms.

Modern mobile tools

Surveyors with smartphones and a data plan can submit the data online.

Export the data, or view it live

You can download your data to Excel with the click of a button. And, you can watch results come in live from the smartphone survey.

You'll know when the data was submitted, and by whom, so you can control the quality of the data

Our roadmap

We're working with community organizations & the City of Detroit to build out this app. We'll be testing it in Summer 2012.

Coders & community groups: we need your help!

Community groups: How can we make this tool best serve your needs? Let us know how & why you're interested, and we'll do our best to help you out.

Developers: We'd love your participation: All our code is on GitHub and our stories are Pivotal Tracker. The mobile app is built on HTML5, Javascript, and Leaflet. Server-side data processing is built on NodeJS, Flask, and PostGIS.

Right now, it's not easy to get all the pieces moving together. We're happy to help with any steps in the process.
